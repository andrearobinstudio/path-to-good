About this chart

To queer contemplative spirituality is to notice that many of the core insights of contemplative traditions — the dissolution of fixed identity, the transgression of boundaries between self and other, the destabilisation of normative categories, the insistence that the sacred exceeds all human attempts to contain it — are themselves profoundly queer in their logic.

Each star carries a spiritual name, a scholarly anchor, a description, and bibliography roots. The three axes represent dimensions of lived human experience: where attention moves (inward or outward), where transformation is in its cycle (dissolution or integration), and what scale of reality is being engaged (the embodied or the transpersonal). Stars that sit close to one another share something. The lines between them are not sequences — they are relationships.

No cult. No surprise. Just the constant journey toward.
